# Skin Care
Aside from slowing general signs of aging (which I think are objectives for most people), my skincare objectives are individualized to me. If you have concerns other than dryness and baggy eyes (such as acne, redness, etc.) you should research those conditions and tailor your objectives, strategies, and tactics.
### Objectives
- Slow general signs of aging
- Reduce dryness
- Reduce under-eye darkness/puffiness
### Strategies
- Slow Aging: Protect against UV
- Slow Aging: Utilize topical antioxidants at clinical significant doses
- Dryness: *WIP*
- Eye darkness: Topical treatments generally follow recommendations for face[1](https://academic.oup.com/asj/article/25/6/618/188803?login=false#1796631)[2](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4924417/)[3](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7992473/#B8)
- Eye puffiness: [Reduce blood flow](https://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/health/treatment-tests-and-therapies/how-to-get-rid-of-bags-under-your-eyes)
- Slow Aging & Dryness: Simplicity --> Consistency
- Slow Aging & Dryness: High availability/low cost --> Consistency
### Tactics
- Protect against UV: *WIP*
- Utilize topical antioxidants at clinical significant doses:

- *WIP Dryness*
- Reduce blood flow: Utilize morning compression (ideally cold) with caffeine
- Simplicity: Do the *same* routine, twice a day, each day of the week. Minimize differences between morning and evening routines.
- High availability/low cost: Select products that meet the above criteria and are available through multiple retailers, with better/best price-per-volume versus comparable products.
