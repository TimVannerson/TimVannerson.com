# Dutch Oven Pulled Pork
An easy, indoor pork shoulder recipe, based on a slow cooker version from my brother-in-law.

If using a slow cooker, sear in pan (preferably cast iron) and set slow cooker to "High".
#### Ingredients 
- [ ] Bone-in pork shoulder butt (generally as small as possible to fit most Dutch ovens/slow cookers)
- [ ] 2Tbsp. flavorless oil (vegetable or avocado)
- [ ] Kosher salt
- [ ] 1-2 Tbsp. *each* garlic powder, onion powder, mustard powder, paprika, cumin, fresh ground black pepper, & brown sugar
- [ ] 1/2 cup BBQ sauce
- [ ] 12 oz. your choice of beer, cider (hard or NA), or stock
- [ ] *Optional*: fresh herbs (thyme, rosemary, bay leaves)

#### Preparation 
1. Cut pork shoulder into 2-4 pieces so it fits in Dutch oven with the lid fully closed
2. Salt on all sides and let sit in fridge 2 hours to overnight (you can use the Dutch oven with the lid off)
3. When ready, preheat oven to 300F, remove pork to cutting board, and wipe out Dutch oven
4. Heat a large drizzle (~2 Tbsp.) of oil on medium-high heat and sear pork on all sides (can go one piece at a time if the Dutch oven is crowded)
5. Rub spice blend on all sides of porkand return to Dutch oven with fat side up
6. Add BBQ sauce and beer/cider/stock and your choice of fresh herbs to Dutch oven with porkl 
7. Cover and cook 3-4 hours until meat pulls apart easily
