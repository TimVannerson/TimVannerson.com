    Hair Loss

    Looking back, I first started losing hair in my very early twenties. I remember washing my hair in the apartment during my last two years of undergrad and noticing a lot of shedding. At the time, I didn't think much of it. Even when my (then) fiancé started to point it out, I brushed things off.

    Later, just after getting married and turning 23, my wife and I were reviewing our wedding photos and I was jarringly disappointed to see how much of my scalp I could see on my crown. I started to take my hair loss more seriously; I talked to a dermatologist and got the common answer: I couldn't reverse the loss but there were things I could to slow or stop further hair loss. I started using minoxidil but prevention ended up not being a strong enough motivator and my use dwindled, then ceased.

    Even as I noticed more hair loss and experienced more self-consciousness about it, I never got serious about treatment again. Ironically, I wish I could go back to the hair in that photo that shocked me eight years ago. However, there is no time like the present and after seeing some (alleged) success stories and reading more into the evidence, I'm giving my hair another shot.

    Hair Growth
    • Structure
      • Shaft: exposed portion of hair
      • Root: portion below skin that goes into dermis
      • Bulb: where stem cells and pigment cells give rise to hair. Capillaries provide nutrients & oxygen to bulb to support cells/new hair growth
    • 3 Phases of Hair Growth
      • Anagen phase: growth phase stem cells give rise to cells that create proteins of hair; 2-8 years for most people on scalp
      • Catagen phase: where hair recedes from bulb towards surface of skin
      • Telogen phase: Bulb pinches off and cell population dies.
    • Accelerators

      - Blood flow (oxygen & nutrients)

    • Inhibitors
    Mechanical Aspect
    • Blood flow: extends anagen phase through increased oxygen and nutrients
    • Follicle stimulation: can sometimes restart cell cycle for telogenic hair niches
    Chemical/Hormonal Aspect
    • IGF-1: regulator that extends anagen phase
      • Growth Hormone released in first few hours of sleep; can be increased with consistent bedtime and no calories 2-3 hours prior
    • Cyclic AMP: also extends anagen phase
    • DHT: an androgen that inhibits IGF1 and Cyclic AMP; converted from testosterone by 5-Alpha Reductase. The primary thing to reduce.
    • PDE: inhibits growth
    • TGF Beta2: inhibits growth

    Treatments

    Minoxidil
    • Oral (prescription): 0.25-5mg/day
    • Topical: 5% 1-2x/day (can get systemic and therefore lower blood pressure)
    Growth Hormone (prescription)
    • Somatropin
    • Semorelin: prescription growth hormone booster
    Insulin Sensitivity boosters
    • Myo-Inositol: 900mg before sleep
    • Berberine: "non-prescription Metformin"
    • Metformin: prescription
    Tadalifil (Cialis)
    • 2.5-5mg
    • Blood flow increase; loss rate slowing
    Saw Palmetto
    • weak 5-Alpha Reductase (converts testosterone to DHT) inhibitor
    • 300mg/day
    Ketoconazole shampoo
    • 2%+; 2-4x/week; 3-5min. contact time
    Caffeine
    • PDE inhibitor
    • 3x/week
    • Minimal research on dosage
    • Not expected to promote new growth
    Finasteride/Duasteride
    • Prescription treatment that has been shown to be generally successful but with significant side effects; monitoring, experimentation, and patience with dosage to mitigate side effects is important
    • 0.01mg/day is associated with 50% reduction in systemic DHT; logarithmic tapering as dosage increases to 5mg/day
    • 0.2-1mg/day is good starting point; given length of anagen phase, should wait up to 6 months before increasing dose
    • Topical treatment: generally shown to have less incidence of side effects; 1mL of 2.5% solution, once per week is generally well tolerated (1% solution ~= 1mg systemic oral dose)
    Rosemary Oil
    • Shown to as effective as 2% minoxidil without side effects (BP reductions, drying of scalp)
    Microneedling

    Summary with many study links - Mechanical stimulation that can reactivate some telogenic hair niches - Good for augmenting hormonal interventions - 1.5-2.5mm needles; 1x/week - 1x/month

    PRP (Pallette Rich Plasma)
    • Expensive and generally only transiently/moderately successful
    • Low priority
    Botox
    • Increases blood flow via release of tension through typical Botox mechanism
    FUE
    • Hair transplant
    • Very expensive and doesn’t necessarily address hair health or growth phases

    • 1mL 2x/day
    • Apply over thinning areas of scalp

    • 1.5-2mm, 1x/week
    • WIP application
    • Koi Beauty Adjustable Derma Stamp

    • 2-3x/week
    • Lather on hands, massage into scalp, leave 3-5 minutes, rinse
    • Intelligent Super Volumizing Shampoo: ketoconazole, caffeine, and saw palmetto, all in one

    • Biotin to help any dryness or brittleness from ketoconazole; not directly supportive of hair growth or loss prevention
    • 2-3x/week, after shampoo
    • Smear onto hair (not scalp), leave 2-3 minutes, rinse
